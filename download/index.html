Install in 4 steps

  1. Download the APK

    Tap the button above on the Android device you want to control.

  2. Allow the install

    Android will ask permission to install from your browser/Files app. Tap Settings → allow "Install unknown apps" for that app, then go back. (One-time, per Android's normal sideload flow.)

  3. Open the file → Install

    Tap the downloaded .apk and confirm Install.

  4. Open ScreenHand & follow setup

    Agree to the disclosure, tap Generate my link (the app creates your connect link + QR right on the device), then enable Accessibility, the keyboard, and battery exemption when prompted. You're online.

Tip: if you don't have a link yet, get one from the home page (Get your link) first.

Is it safe?

  • Signed by us. Every build is signed by Clazro Technology Private Limited — verify the SHA-256 above matches your download.
  • You're in control. ScreenHand only runs after you enable it, and you can stop the worker or turn off Accessibility any time.
  • Token-isolated. Your device sits behind your own personal link; nobody else's link can reach it.
  • No hidden behavior. ScreenHand uses Android's Accessibility Service to read the screen and tap/type on your behalf — we disclose this up front and ask for consent before it turns on. See our Privacy Policy and Terms.
  • Honest disclosure. When you connect an AI agent, your screen contents are sent to that provider so it can act — ScreenHand is not local-only. Only run it on a device you're comfortable having observed/controlled.

Requirements

• Android 9.0 (Pie) or newer
• A ScreenHand link (from Get your link)
• An MCP-compatible AI client to drive it (Claude Code, Claude Desktop, Cursor…) — or just the browser live view.
